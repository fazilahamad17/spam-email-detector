About the Project

Learn how machine learning keeps our digital communication secure.

📨 What is Spam?

Spam refers to unwanted or unsolicited bulk messages sent electronically. While often just annoying advertisements, spam messages can also carry phishing links, malware, or scams designed to compromise your personal and financial security.

🤖 What is Machine Learning?

Machine Learning (ML) is a branch of Artificial Intelligence that allows computers to learn patterns from data without being explicitly programmed. Instead of writing complex rules to catch spam, we show the computer thousands of examples of both spam and normal messages, and it learns to distinguish them on its own.

🔍 How This Project Works

This website uses a statistical model called Multinomial Naive Bayes. First, text is converted into numbers using a technique called TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency), which calculates how important a word is in a message. The model calculates the mathematical probability of a message being spam based on the words it contains.

📊 The Dataset

We trained our algorithm on the popular SMS Spam Collection Dataset. It contains over 5,500 real-world messages, labeled as "ham" (legitimate) or "spam". This allows our model to achieve high accuracy (above 96%) on typical messages.
